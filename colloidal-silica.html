What Is Electronic Grade Colloidal Silica?

A stable aqueous or non-aqueous dispersion of discrete, amorphous silicon dioxide nanoparticles, manufactured to the highest purity standards for use in semiconductor and precision electronics processes.

Electronic grade colloidal silica is a stable, near-monodisperse suspension of amorphous SiO₂ nanoparticles in aqueous media (or select organic solvents), produced through controlled hydrolysis and condensation of ultra-high purity silicon precursors.

Unlike fumed silica or precipitated silica, colloidal silica particles are spherical, discrete, and non-aggregated — characteristics that are critical for reproducible Chemical Mechanical Planarization (CMP) of silicon wafers, sapphire substrates, compound semiconductors, and precision optical surfaces.

Electronic grade specifications demand sub-ppb levels of metallic impurities (Na⁺, K⁺, Fe³⁺, Al³⁺, Cu²⁺) and tightly controlled particle size distributions to prevent micro-scratching of device-quality surfaces. Our proprietary ion-exchange purification delivers consistent, batch-verified purity.

Available in particle sizes from 5 nm to 150 nm, SiO₂ concentrations of 5% to 50% (w/w), and pH ranges tailored to acidic (CMP oxide), neutral, and alkaline (CMP STI/W) process chemistries.

// End-Use Markets

Applications

From front-end semiconductor fabrication to precision optics and advanced coatings, colloidal silica is a critical process material across industries.

🔬

CMP — Semiconductor Wafer Polishing

Primary abrasive in CMP slurries for silicon wafers, STI, ILD, W-plug, Cu interconnect, and barrier layer planarization. Controls removal rate and achieves sub-Angstrom surface roughness (Ra < 0.1 nm).

💡

Sapphire & LED Substrate Finishing

Final polishing of sapphire wafers for LED epitaxy and SiC substrates for power electronics, delivering epi-ready surface quality required for defect-free device growth.

🔭

Optical & Photonic Substrates

Scratch-free finishing of fused silica, BK7 glass, UV optics, laser mirrors, and precision lenses. Achieves surface roughness compatible with DUV and EUV lithography.

🧪

Anti-Reflection Coatings (ARC)

Porous silica ARC films formed from colloidal silica for solar panels, display cover glass, camera lenses, and optical filters — achieving reflectance < 0.5%.

📐

Hard Coatings & Scratch Resistance

Nano-silica filled UV-curable topcoats for plastic optics, automotive HMI displays, smartphone cover glass, and polycarbonate glazing offering pencil hardness > 6H.

⚡

Dielectric & Insulation Films

Spin-on silica precursor for interlayer dielectric (ILD) gap-fill, STI fill, and passivation layers in CMOS logic and memory device manufacturing.

🔋

Battery & Energy Materials

Silica-coated battery separators, SiO₂ additive for silicon-carbon anode composites, and binder in solid electrolyte ceramic processing for next-generation energy storage.

🏗️

Ceramic Binder & Precision Casting

Colloidal silica binder for investment casting shells, refractory coatings, and advanced technical ceramics — providing green-body strength and controlled sintering.

// Key Differentiators

Technical Advantages

Why manufacturers specify electronic grade colloidal silica over alternatives.

  • Discrete Spherical Morphology

    Non-aggregated, near-spherical particles provide isotropic polishing contact, minimising micro-scratching and achieving superior surface finish compared to fumed or precipitated silica.

  • Narrow Particle Size Distribution

    Coefficient of variation (CV) < 15% for most grades; critical for controlling removal rate uniformity and within-wafer non-uniformity (WIWNU) in CMP applications.

  • Long-Term Colloidal Stability

    High negative zeta potential (–30 to –60 mV) provides electrostatic repulsion preventing particle agglomeration, ensuring 24+ months shelf life when stored properly.

  • Ultra-Low Metallic Ion Content

    Sub-ppb Na⁺, Fe³⁺, Al³⁺, Cu²⁺ levels achieved through multi-stage ion exchange, preventing device contamination in FEOL semiconductor processes.

  • Tunable pH & Surface Chemistry

    Available across acidic to alkaline pH ranges; surface hydroxyl density adjusted for specific substrate selectivity ratios and integration with oxidants, inhibitors, and surfactants.

  • Oversize Particle Control

    Liquid particle counting (LPC) certification ensures < 50 particles/mL > 0.5 μm in ultra-pure grades, preventing killer defects in advanced node device fabrication.
